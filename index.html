2007
  • Developed my very first blog.
  • Troublemaker and struggling academic.
  • Suspended for treason and cyberbullying.

I was an unconfident child.

My self-esteem would sop like a Siberian jelly, veering off the plate and into the delicious chalice of superiority complex entwined with my soul. I was heavily unadjusted for reality, feigning curiosity upon the unmet expectations of my family; bleeding tar under the guise of industrial espionage.

Life wasn't difficult, so much that it was contrived. Growing up with a string of father figures ranging from the incompetent to the disorderly, all that was left to abhor was my mother - an irrational judgement of backhanded self-criticism and conditional hugs. You know, because empty tears should play an essential role in any growing boy's diet.

In 2007, a traditional diet of rice and pasta is reinventing the definition of cellulite on my face. My hair is as greasy a mechanic's handshake and my eyes are barely attempting to persevere through the severe pornography addiction shaking my reality. I was just another kid with the expectations of an investment banker, pretending to do his best whilst utterly chaotic in a jumbled mess of confusion and spontaneous hostility.

I wasn't a particularly dedicated or passionate student. All I really cared about was playing video games, delegating what little responsibility I had towards cultivating the teenage fantasy of cheap women, draping what little promiscuity they have in the loose attitudes sweating from their busts; a pinnacle for those too terrified to even look a girl in the eye.

Aside from the creepy posterior and discarded euphoria, I hated absolutely everyone, including myself. I would spend each lunchtime in the library, hiding away from the world, feeding off the moisture manifesting from my avid self-deprivation. "The delicate hand of life is prying at my misery, burying memories at a haste not last seen since the Great Depression."

Fortunately, years of self-hate and brooding would develop into a coping mechanism sparing me the pity of my visible distress, both physically and mentally: My ability to make people laugh. My humour, which was as dry as crushed oregano, would allow me to mask the inconsistency of my mood and behaviour, as a kind of quirk to be admired. I was even offered to become part of the jock and theatre groups following a string of particularly hilarious events, to which I arrogantly declined in dramatic fashion.

I suppose my first attempt at media began with an online forum some kids in our year level had decided to propagate. It was very bootleg at the time, causing mischief for those who desired it. Naturally, as part of the ingrown tumor accommodating the seizure prone area of my brain, I thought it appropriate to create my own rival forum. Obviously, it would be bigger and better in every way, cultivating pride with grant hysteria.

I'll spare you the details, however I ended up being suspended, with both treason and cyberbullying sighted as key reasons.

So instead, to spare myself the future embarrassment of being a rampant liar, I decided to start a personal blog to note down my thoughts and feelings, entitled Adelaidesucks. Why? Because there was a kid from Adelaide and like a pack of hyenas, felt obliged to make him feel death for it. If we had a shooting range, we would have crippled instead.

Despite being a terrible writer with a mild case of dyslexia and an inability to decipher between past and present tense, it was a harmless activity which would spur my love for writing and humour.

2008
  • Successful Myspace spam career.
  • Developed a habit of recording myself.
  • Blogging du jour ala sucks.
  • First venture into YouTube and video media.

With no friends and an awkward attitude, bordering on a kind of ritual patheticism no one ever dares to admit, I managed to develop a habit of talking to myself.

It was a way for me to keep the heart warm and flood the soul with a temporary diaspora, keeping what little hope I had in the teenage dream alive. Then next step, obviously, was to buy an audio recorder to keep the voices permanent. I would record zany shows, stupid voices aaaaaaand just about anything to remind myself that everything was perfectly fine, in-spite of the violation and atrophy lurking around the corner.

Though really, it gets a lot worse. To further validate my self-worth, I would create ‘funny’ MySpace accounts, spamming random people to provide their opinions on whether they found it the most brilliantly hilarious thing they've ever read on earth. Often reinforcing my delusions of grandeur, I would then proceed to feel amazing about myself, as I'm sure we've all desired at some point in our lives.

In 2008 I also experimented with video media for the first time, inspired by YouTube pop culture. I decided to create a somewhat unoriginal video game/music video compilation for dottjt my very first YouTube channel. The video was a montage of Super Smash Brothers' footage to the song "Smack That" by Akon. Truly, it was the best my 15 year old brain could manage.

I also continued blogging, creating MelbourneSucks, SydneySucks, HobartSucks and PerthSucks and in fact, an appropriately entitled blog for each Australian major city. Please don't ask why, it's just what kids do.

2009
  • Hosted my very first radio show at the age of sixteen.
  • Became obsessed with podcasting.
  • Sexting gone wrong.

Eventually it dawned on me that babbling incoherently into a recorder simply wasn’t enough. Listening to a lot of podcasts at the time (particularly Get This, featuring a young Ed Kavalee and The Spoonman on Triple M), I was keen to become a radio professional, and so I applied for a radio show at SYN FM.

Thankfully my application was approved and my very first radio show Just Another Topical Afternoon was born. Presenting with a friend from high school, I wrote and produced the whole thing, while he was blessed with turning up infrequently, often stoned, eventually not turning up at all.

Aside from the visceral disagreements and choking fights I had with the station's general manager, I soon became obsessed with my amateur hobby, espousing bullshit for a pretty dime. After each show, I would quickly rush home to edit the studio recording, to which I would sit and listen on repeat, thinking it was the most magical thing. Hundreds, upon hundreds of times.

Perhaps the most exciting aspect of producing radio was recieving on-air texts from listeners. There was an on-going joke on the show that you could win a date with me, which wasn't actually a joke, but a desperate ploy for a sexually hookup. Eventually, someone bit. Later admitting "she" was suicidal, I freaked out, only to be told that this suicidal teenager was actually an undercover cop doing research, having called the number to the voice of a man in an office.

More viably, I was the victim of a convincing prank, though would certainly not be the last I used my media talents to try and attract women.

2010
  • Experimented with YouTube comedy.

I didn't spend a lot of time creating things in my last year of high school, as I was too busy pretending to study, whilst actually playing Pokemon on my Nintendo DS. Following exams however, I had a clear idea of what I wanted to do: I wanted to become the next big thing on YouTube.

YouTube was beginning to gain traction as a platform and with the likes of mychonny and other self-obsessed personalities, I wanted to make others laugh. Collaborating with a entirely different, and more sober friend from high school, we decided to create a YouTube channel entitled Back Hand Films, which at one point features a young Julius Reade wearing a corset, whilst being tea-bagged like a wet fish. We created videos and performed improv.

Unfortunately, the outfit wasn’t destined to work out. I didn’t think he was funny and he thought I was too much of a control freak. This not only ended our YouTube venture, however also our somewhat incompatible friendship. My dream was in ruins.

2011
  • SYN FM, 3SSR, Radio Monash and Vision Australia Radio.
  • Began passion for finance and economics via The Financial Reality.
  • Started music/comedy website.
  • Wrote a radio play.

Enrolled in a Bachelor of Journalism at Monash University, I would soon further my involvement in community radio to fulfil my self-worth, following the failure of my YouTube comedy ambitions. At one point, I was on four different radio networks at the same time, extorting my pride like a crack pipe connected deep into the manic depression I thought candour.

At Radio Monash and 3SSR I partnered with my best friend at the time, presenting and producing The Faptactular One Hour Special and CRACK thinking it was going to be some kind of magical Hamish and Andy experience. Unfortunately, it wasn’t. It turned out that he wasn’t really into the whole radio thing and with a wistful haste, the duo was in tatters.

Hoping to regain some kind of common ground, we instead decided to start a website called We Talk, You Die which would feature both music and comedy articles and videos. Of course, the two didn’t really mix and as my cynicism for journalism grew, so did my involvement wane, eventually phasing myself out entirely altogether.

SYN FM was a different story. Whilst producing my radio show, Hidden Controversy, I met a future collaborator who I would later produce another show with entitled The Hourly Wage which I announced and he panelled. I would continue to collaborate with him to produce and record a radio play I had written, which after one successful initial recording, would proceed to bail on my project and produce his own series of podcasts. Naturally, we stopped being friends.

As you can tell, none of my collaborations work out.

At the same time, I was also volunteering at Vision Australia Radio to read articles and produce a youth radio show entitled Don't Hold Back. I would end up staying there for more than three years, producing and presenting more than 100 episodes of Don’t Hold Back. Of course, not that I enjoyed the experience. It simply looked good on my resume.

In 2011 I was also getting pretty involved with economics and finance. I found topics fascinating beyond belief and as a journalism hopeful, was learning quite a lot about the corruption in finance. This lead to me creating my YouTube channel The Financial Reality which would aim to explain complex financial issues in layman's terms. Following many sleepless nights, I eventually managed to produce a nine minute video explaining the global debt crisis, which to this day has more than 150,000 views.

Please don’t watch it. It makes me cringe like a piece of burnt plastic, waving for mercy.

2012
  • Decided to give YouTube comedy another crack, unsuccessfully.
  • Continued finance involvement via the Zero Hedge Daily Round Up podcast.
  • Begun the Drudge Drudge Report podcast.
  • Wrote terrible poetry whilst on drugs.

Early in the year I had an idea for a podcast which I thought would be quite successful.

As a self-confessed Zero Hedge fanatic (before it lost the plot), I thought there would be a media opportunity to summarise all the news items on the website, as well as provide my own take on them. The end result was The Zero Hedge Daily Round Up, a six minute finance/comedy news bulletin that took three hours to produce each Tuesday to Saturday. I would wake up at 7:00am and have the podcast finished at 10:00am, just in time for New York audiences.

It ended up being highly successful, with more than 55,000 downloads in total. I was even featured as a regular contributor on the Zero Hedge website itself! Unfortunately, despite the success of my podcast, the Zero Hedge crowd disliked my humour and ousted me as a clown. It was disheartening to think the community I'd embraced thought I was a complete twat, so I stopped producing the podcast in late October.

Of course, there was a greater problem. My depression and mental health issues were beginning to exacerbate and take hold of my life. All the pressure I had put myself under of becoming a success, weighed by the failures of my past, were beginning to tear into the fabric of my self-perception. I simply couldn't keep up with my own demands.

I stopped being consistent with my media. I would produce for weeks at a time, only to crash and burn into a horrible pit of agony and self-worthlessness. I would criticise myself, blaming my failure on my inability to just. be. better. In my arrogant stupor, I assumed my failure and worthless was due to not trying hard enough, which would prompt an even stronger response, only to crash and burn out more spectacularly each time.

One of these responses was to create a second podcast in tandem with my existing podcast entitled Drudge Report Report which was a similar concept, however targeted at a much larger and more influential media outlet, which rapidly grew in popularity.

On the YouTube front I decided to mix my two passions: finance and comedy. I would create these comedy videos which would last from late-2012 to mid-2013: The Financial Reality 2012/2013.

It begs to say that I also spent a large portion of 2012 writing terrible, somewhat sexual poetry whilst being high on drugs. I distinctly remember recording a few episodes of the round up whilst tripping on LSD. I'd also travelled to France, England and China during the year.

I also dedcided to give YouTube comedy again another chance via Juliusismyname. As you can imagine, it didn't work out, what with my crippling mental health issues.

2013
  • The passionless husk of my depression begins to bloom.
  • Ingenious plan to use tumblr for women.
  • Which of course, fuels the depression.
  • Though, my writing improves in leaps and strides.

Then one day I just stopped.

I stopped producing media altogether and gave up my ambitions of becoming the next big thing on YouTube or iTunes or anywhere. I was entirely broken and distraught, wanting nothing more than to fade away and pretend that the whole experience was just a forgettable dream, creating agony in a world far, far away.

It’s hard to describe mental illness, however I couldn’t quite deal with the guilt and shame of burning out and disappearing for weeks at a time.

I felt judged and vindicated, feeling like a complete and utter failure. All those broken collaborations and friends lost. Not to mention, the disaster that was university; a sopping failure of poor grades and pointless intuition. Nothing in my life seemed to be working out, and so I stopped. I had no reason to continue.

It may also help to understand what it's like growing up with an Asian mother. You see, Asian mothers don't provide positive reinforcement. Instead, what they do is judge and criticise you as a form of motivation. Instead of praising you for what you did right, they instead point out the thing you did wrong and ultimately, that is all you ever focus on: all the things you do wrong. A recipe for disaster, which would mould into the psyche attacking me from all angles.

But rather than truly give up and let go of all the pain and misdirection in my heart, I did what any unknowningly desperate person would do: chase one hopeless ambition for another, like a gambling addict chuffing down on a cigarette to mitigate the loses from a previous night of dopamine shuffle.

I decided to pursue a different goal, a different rush instead: Women.

I knew zero women at the time and with zero sexual experience, thought it was a worthy challenge to tackle. As I knew absolutely no women what-so-ever, I had to be inventive, which lead me towards Tumblr.

I thought Tumblr would be good for a number of reasons. For starters, it wasn't a dating website, so it would subtle. Not to mention, the girls on tumblr were usually mentally ill and as someone who also had problems of his own, though it would be a good fit. In addition, the way I would attract would would be via my writing and so I would have an expressive outlet, as well as an audience. Brilliant, I thought.

This lead to my first blog inkquotes. The idea was that I would handwrite quotes and girls would find them pretty. Initially, it was a bit of a disaster, however after one month of painstaking effort, I finally managed to find a hookup and within two months, I secured my first girlfriend. I was over the moon like you cannot believe, later creating tktxtktx during our short relationship.

Of course, what they don’t tell you about love is that it’s a horrible idea to become attached to someone who is mentally ill and suicidal, so naturally the relationship broke down and my mental health collapsed like the twin towers.

I overdosed on a variety of drugs. I distinctly remember screaming in the middle of the street as the heavy rain pierced through my skull, as the entire neighbourhood came out to watch the fool entrapped in a psychotic comatose. As the police approached me with their guns held firmly, I begged for them to shoot, but instead cuffed me and took me to hospital, with my arm damaged from trying to punch in a glass door.

I thought maybe it would get her attention, or something. Who really knows why we do the things we do. Sometimes we just want to feel loved.

I think I'm crying.

2014
  • Sickle despair.
  • Write more than 170,000 words in an obsessive haste.
  • Quit tumblr to focus on my mental health, sort of.

It's unfortunate to think that my depression would get worse before it would get better.

I became more and more irrational, frequently trading one bad crowd for another. I was highly insecure and an emotional wreck at the best of times. I was smoking weed and taking research chemicals without consequence. I failed to value myself and would do anything women said.

I vividly remember breaking down at work. I was unpacking a pallet of various liquids and I remember picking up a bottle of gardening poison and thinking: "What possibly difference would it make if I just swallowed the whole thing right now?" It was a low point and the first and last suicidal thought I'd ever had.

I mean, what kind of a 21 year old hangings around 16 year old LADS in bad parts of town? Well, the internet, apparently. Drug dealers, arsonists and delinquents. I remember one kid named porky who was 15 and completely covered in tattoos. He got aggressive when I poked fun at his mother, however the confidence impressed him. Part of me was attracted to the danger. I thought maybe this is what it meant to be a journalist.

The good news is that the insecurity, the drugs, the near death experiences, all made for incredibly interesting and brilliant writing. It helped fuel the content for my tumblr blogs, convincing women I was some kind of adventurous William S. Burroughs, my idol at the time. I would go on to create sweetcherry erotica, blackwhiteinkcurves and brokenheartsandfracturedsmiles writing in excess of 170,000 words.

Of course, I knew I couldn’t continue this life of arbitrary mischievous forever. Following a string of bad women, I pledged to get off tumblr and instead focus solely on my mental health and break the cycle of my insecurity. It wasn’t easy. I ended up trading one addiction for another, soon turning to gambling and pornography to ease my woes.

When I left however, there was one girl I still wanted to keep in contact with. She’d claimed to have already had a boyfriend when I last spoke to her, so I thought she might make a nice friend. So we added each other on Facebook and within 5 minutes, I instantly knew we would be in a relationship (it turns out she was single) and we’ve been together ever since.

To celebrate my love for her, I wrote her a small book for Christmas. It’s a few hundred pages, packed full of drawings, cartoons, jokes and pictures. I took me way too long to make.

2015
  • Full-time working career begins, reluctantly.
  • Perspective Theory launched to the masses.
  • Officially ends thy tumblr blogging.

The rebound.

Graduating at the end of 2014, I reluctantly began my venture into full-time work. Aside from my depression and mental health issues, there were also no journalism/writing jobs available for my experience. In addition, I was beginning to doubt the authenticity of journalism as a career.

Thankfully, I had a plan. I was going to develop a writing portfolio so that by 2016, I could get a writing/journalism position. In the meantime, I would work full-time in various "off cut" positions to bathe myself in a wide variety of work experiences. That writing portfolio was to become Perspective Theory, a website to help those suffering from depression.

As I’d been actively working on my mental health over the years, I’d read enough about perspective and self-awareness to understand how it worked. At the same time, I couldn't get it to consistently work, so I thought maybe if I were to write about it, I'd get better at managing my mental health. Ultimately, I ended up publishing more than 90,000 words, writing a hell of a lot more.

My first job was as an Education Consultant at ProLearn selling TAFE education. It was one of the most horrendous experiences of my life. Put simply, the turnover was so bad that you would see 30 new faces each week. Half would be gone by the next day and you'd maybe have a handful left by the end of the week.

The experience was so traumatic that it took me another two months before I could garner the courage to find another position, which lead me to discover a Digital Marketing Specialist position I was clearly unqualified for (I distinctly remember the MD shouting at me over my « terrible » email newsletter).

Afterwards I discovered a Marketing Assistant position at OzWashroom - an operation which claimed to be the second largest washroom supplier nationally. Of course, I managed to get fired after three weeks.

You see, in preparing for marketing positions, I was required to learn a lot of HTML/CSS and web design. As a result, I virtually spent all my time learning about HTML/CSS and JavaScript, which I became virtually obsessed with. I would spend all my free time coding... particularly at work. Eventually I got caught out and I was accused of hacking into their system to steal, in their words, their "SEO" as I was put it.

Now halfway through the year, I realised that I probably had enough experience to secure a proper writing position. Delivering a series of impressive interviews, I ended up as a Content Writer at Expotrade, a company responsible for a monopoly share of major project and transport infrastructure projects within the state. Actually, my title was immediately upgraded to a PR & Communications Executive, which couldn’t have sounded more impressive, at least on paper.

I also prompted started another tumblr blog elegantlyattached before realising that a) already had a wonderful girlfriend and b) there were probably more important things I could do with my time.

2016
  • Manage to sustain full-time employment for more than two months.
  • Studies very, very hard to learn computer science.
  • One last crack at YouTube comedy.

After much struggle I finally managed to get my mental health in order. I cut off all the toxic people in my life, including my own mother, and I managed to become mentally stable, which is weird. I don’t feel like it’s inherently better, just more in line with my own goals.

I don’t take my emotions or episodes personally. I very seldom get burnt out despite working twice as hard as I ever have, and most importantly, I no longer feel riddled with guilt or shame about who I am. My mind is simply calm and focused. It’s like my brain has been upgraded or something.

One of the big worries of mental stability is thinking that your ambition and character will disappear. However, I can ascertain that it only gets strong. To actually have control over yourself is like being given a gun, over a spear; control to be as functioning or as mentally ill as you like, but control none-the-less.

With my mental health in order, I decided to give YouTube comedy one last crack: The Financial Reality 2015/2016 While it started out fine, soon evolved into pure hate and and incoherence, almost by design. Regardless, it wasn’t contributing towards my mental health and so I stopped. Was it funny? I'm not sure.

If I had to sum up everything I've done this year, I would say I spent a majority of this year studying code.
