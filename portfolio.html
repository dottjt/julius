2007
  • Developed my very first blog Adelaidesucks.
  • Troublemaker and struggling academic.
  • Got suspended for treason and cyberbullying.

I was a shit kid.

I wasn't a particularly dedicated or passionate student and aside from the loose attitude, my superiority complex was so thick you could hack it with a machette and drain the tar from my skull. I hated everyone, including myself. I would spend each lunchtime in the library, hiding away from the world, feeding off the moisture manifesting from my own self-deprivation.

On a positive note however, I was also funny. It was a skill which spared me the pity of being visibly distressed, both mentally and physically. I was even offered to become part of the jock and theatre groups following a string of particularly hilarious events, to which I arrogantly declined in dramatic fashion. Apparently, horrible didn't quite cut it out for me.

I suppose my first attempt at media began with an online forum some kids in our year level had decided to create. It was very bootleg. Naturally, the seizure prone area of my brain decided to create a rival forum, which would obviously aim to be better in every single way. I'll spare you the details, however I ended up being suspended. Both treason and cyberbullying were sighted as key reasons.

Instead, I decided to start a personal blog as I thought it would be far less detrimental on my academic life, entitled Adelaidesucks. Why? Because there was a kid from Adelaide and it was obligatory that we make it known to him of his worthlessness, teachers included too.

Even though I was a terrible writer with a mild case of dyslexia and an inability to descipher between past and present sentence structure, it was an enjoyable pastime.

2009
  • Hosted my very first radio show at the age of sixteen.
  • Became obsessed with podcasting.
  • Sexting gone wrong.

It eventually dawned on me that babbling incoherently into a recorder simply wasn’t enough. Listening to a lot of podcasts at the time (particularly Get This, featuring a young Ed Kavalee and The Spoonman on Triple M), I was particularly keen to become a radio professional, so I applied for my very own show at SYN FM.

Thankfully my application was approved and my very first radio show, Just Another Topical Afternoon, was born. Presenting with a friend from high school, I wrote and produced the whole thing, while he was assigned to turning up infrequently, often stoned, eventually not turning up at all.

Aside from the visceral disagreements and fights I would have with the general manager of the station, which I vehemently protested, I soon discovered I was literally obsessed with producing radio. After each show, I would quickly rush home to edit the studio recording, where I would sit and listen to the tape on repeat, hundreds upon hundreds of times. I thought it was magical.

Perhaps the most exciting aspect of producing radio is recieveing on-air texts from listeners. There was an on-going joke on the show that you could win a date with me, which soon became reality when a person offered their number. Later admitting "she" was suicidal, I freaked out, only to be told later that the person was actually an undercover cop doing research. More viably, I was the victim of a prank.

2010
  • Began my venture into YouTube comedy with a friend.
  • Of course, it didn't work out.

I don’t think I spent a lot of time creating things in my last year of high school, as I was probably too busy pretending to study, whilst actually secretly playing Pokemon Diamond on my Nintendo DS. Well, that and doing stupid voices to myself. Following my exams however, I had a clear idea of what I wanted to do: I wanted to become a YouTube star.

YouTube was beginning to gain traction as a platform and with the likes of mychonny and other self-obsessed personalities, I wanted to become a household name. Collaborating with a friend from high school, we decided to make a YouTube Channel called BackHand Films, which was as racist and sexist as it sounds. We created scripted videos, as well as a lot of improv.

Unfortunately, the outfit didn’t work out. I didn’t think he was funny and he thought I was too much of a control freak. In retrospect, I think we both realised that we were too individual to be able to work as a team, with both of us wanted to be leaders in our own realm, rather than collaborate. This not only ended our YouTube venture, however also our friendship. My dream was in ruins.

2011
  • University Radio at 3SSR, Radio Monash.
  • Community Radio at Vision Australia Radio.
  • SYN FM, Hidden Controversy, The Hourly Wage.
  • Started We Talk, You Die written and audio content.
  • Started The Financial Reality YouTube Channel, releasing Global Debt Crisis video.

Following the tragedy of my YouTube comedy ambitions, I decided to turn to radio to fulfil my self-worth. Enrolled in a Bachelor of Journalism at Monash University, I would soon further my involvement in community radio. At one point, I was on four different radio networks at the same time - SYN FM, Radio Monash, 3SSR and Vision Australia Radio.

At Radio Monash and 3SSR I partnered with my best friend at the time, thinking it was going to be some kind of magical Hamish and Andy experience. Unfortunately, it wasn’t. It turned out that he wasn’t really into the whole radio thing and that he was much more interested in doing music journalism, and so the duo was in tatters, as would be our friendship into the future.

Hoping to regain some kind of common ground, we instead decided to start a website called We Talk, You Die (well, it was largely his doing) which would feature both music and comedy articles and videos. Of course, the two didn’t really mix and so I decided to phase out my involvement with the whole thing. Eventually, it ended up to become a successful indie music website, though we no longer speak.

SYN FM was a different story. Whilst producing my show, Hidden Controversy, I met a future collaborator who I would later produce another show with, The Hourly Wage, which I announced and he panelled. Having also written a radio play later in the year, I would collaborate with him to produce and record the production. After a successful initial recording, he basically bailed to produce his own series of podcasts. As you can tell, none of my collaborations ever seem to work out.

Vision Australia Radio was a different story. It was a radio station of the blind, which I would read articles n’ stuff. Leaving early 2015, I managed to produce and present more than 100 episodes of Don’t Hold Back, a sort of youth radio show, which arguably I didn’t enjoy, but thought would look good on my resume.

At the same time, I was getting pretty involved with economics and finance in my life. I simply found it fascinating and as a journalism hopeful, I was learning quite a lot about the corruption in finance. As a result I decided to make a Financial YouTube Channel that would explain complex financial issues in simple terms, entitled The Financial Reality. With a lot of sleepless nights, I eventually managed to produce a nine minute video explaining the global debt crisis, which to this day has more than 150,000 views.

Please don’t watch it. It makes me cringe, bad time.

2012
  • 2nd Attempt at YouTube comedy.
  • Begun Zero Hedge Daily Round Up podcast.
  • Traveled abroad to France and China for an extended period of time.
  • Started getting highly political and philosophical.
  • Wrote terrible poetry and started experimenting with hard drugs.

Following the release of my global debt crisis video, I had an idea for a podcast, which I thought would be quite successful. I was a Zero Hedge fanatic at the time (before it went crazy) and I thought, what if I created a daily round up of all of Zero Hedge’s daily news events? The end result would become six a six minutes finance/comedy daily bulletin that took 3 hours to produce each morning, each Tuesday to Saturday. I would wake up at 7am to make the thing by 5pm NYT. It ended up being highly successful, with more than 55,000 downloads. I was even featured a regular contributor spot on the Zero Hedge Website itself!

Unfortunately, there was a problem. Despite the success of my podcast, the Zero Hedge crowd hated me. They disliked my humour. They thought I was childish, which I was. It was disheartening to think the community I had embraced thought I was a complete twat. Though there was another greater problem. My depression and mental illness issues was failing to keep up with my ambitions. While my mental health was never particularly great, it was now exacerbated by my media commitments, my desire for fame and the failure of my collaborations.

I could never be consistent. I would produce media for weeks and then crash and burn into a horrible pit of depression and self-worthlessness. Sometimes I’d stop producing shows for weeks at a time. My response, as a result of my upbringing, was to try hard and criticise myself further for not being good enough, you know, how my mother brought me up. I thought my depression was due to a lack of content creation, and so I would come back harder and stronger.

For The Financial Reality YouTube Channel however, I decided to go down a different route. I decided to mix my two passions together, finance and comedy. The first video I created was called Ben Bernanke Tells Us About money, which I still think is hilarious to this day. In October 2012 I focused solely on creating financial comedy videos until the middle of 2013.

When I think about it, I also spent a large portion of 2012 writing terrible, somewhat sexual poetry. I also messed around with hard drugs, as a distinctly remember recording a few episodes of the Zero Hedge Daily Round Up while on LSD. I’d also travelled to China and France during the middle of the year. Oh, and at some point I decided to give YouTube comedy a second crack. Though thanks to my mental health, barely got past 4 episodes before deducing that I was a worthless piece of crap.

The last few months of The Zero Hedge Daily Round Up I decided to start a new podcast, The Drudge Report Report, which would assume the same concept of the financial podcast, however target a much bigger media outlet and focus more on comedy. While the popularity grew rapidly, I eventually crashed to a point where I could no longer keep going. My mental illness was destroying me.

It’s hard to describe, but I couldn’t deal with the guilt and shame of burning out and disappearing into the void. When I was to return, I felt judged and vindicated. The constant criticism made me feel like a complete and utter failure. In addition, I felt like my life WAS a failure. All those failed collaborations. All those friends lost. It seemed like nothing was working out, so I quit. I stopped producing media altogether. Not to mention, I was absolutely abhoring university.

But rather than give up and let go of all the pain and misdirection, I decided to do something that was arguably worse. Chasing one hopeless ambition for another, I decided to pursue a different goal instead: Women. I don’t know what sparked it, but I was determined to get a girlfriend. Hell, I was 20 and I hadn’t even kissed a girl yet, so I knew that was something I wanted.

I had a brilliant thought. What if I used tumblr to pursue women? I mean, a heap of women were on tumblr and because it’s not a dating site, it would subtle and thus perfect. Not to mention, the girls on tumblr were usually mentally ill and so we’d probably click. Of course, the only it was going to work is if I had a gimmick, so I decided I would handwrite quotes on my blog, in addition to my daily thoughts.

Initially, it was a bit of a disaster, however after one month and a lot of internet stalking, I finally managed to kiss a girl and within two months, I had my very first girlfriend. Of course, what they don’t tell you is that it’s a horrible idea to fall in love with someone else who is also mentally ill and suicidal, so naturally the relationship broke down and I sunk into an incredibly depressed. I overdosed on a variety of drugs and ended up in hospital. It was the beginning of my relative downfall.

2015
  • Ozwashroom - Marketing Assistant.
  • McLeod Rail - Digital Marketing Specialist.
  • ProLearn - Education Consultant.
  • Created Perspective Theory, a digital website to assist those with depression.

I suppose this year was somewhat of a rebound. Graduating at the end of 2014, I began my venture into full-time work. There were a lot of challenges ahead, however I had no choice but to take it one day at a time. Aside from my depression and mental health issues, there were also no journalism/writing jobs available for my experience and given my experience at university, was unsure whether journalism was my thing.

Thankfully, I had a plan. I was going to develop a writing portfolio during 2015, so that by 2016, I could get the kinds of jobs I wanted. In the meantime, I would work full-time in various positions to sort of get a wide variety of experiences. That writing portfolio was to become Perspective Theory, a website to help those suffering from depression. As I’d been actively working on my mental health over the years, I’d read enough about perspective and self-awareness to understand how it worked, yet I didn’t quite « get it » . So I thought if I were to write about it, I could better understand how it functioned, as well as develop my own theories and systems. Ultimately, I ended up writing more than 90,000 words (more like 200,000 if you include unpublished drafts).

As for my working career, I spent two months at a tele-marketing place which was horrendous. Put simply, the turnover was so bad that you would have 30 people would enter the company each week and only two people would stay. I’m sure I could write an article on it, however it was soul destroying. It took me another two months before I could garner the courage to find another position, which lead me to discover a Digital Marketing Specialist position I was clearly unqualified for (I distinctly remember the MD shouting at me over my « terrible » email newsletter).

Following that venture I managed to score a Marketing Assistant position at Ozwashroom - a small operation which claimed to be a the second largest washroom supplier nationally. Given my sensibilities, I managed to get myself fired after three weeks.

You see, in preparing for marketing positions, I was required to learn a lot of HTML/CSS and web design. As a result, I virtually spent all my time learning about HTML/CSS, which eventually lead to learning about JavaScript and code. I loved learning code so much that I became obsessed. I would spend all my free time coding, particularly at work. Eventually I got caught out. The funny thing is that they thought I was actually hacking into their system (I won’t comment on the intelligence at the company) for trying to steal their « SEO », as he put it.

Now halfway through the year, I realised that I probably had enough experience to secure a proper writing position. Delivering a series of impressive interviews, I ended up as a Content Creator at Expotrade - a global conferencing firm. Actually, my title was immediately upgraded to a PR & Communications Executive, which couldn’t sound more amazing. I would write all their collateral, including press releases and news pieces, as well as attend events to interview speakers and ministers.

Don’t worry, it only sounds impressive on paper.

2016
  • Started writing articles on Medium.
  • Finished up my position at Expotrade.
  • Gave YouTube comedy one last crack.

What a beautiful year. After much struggle I finally managed to get my mental health in order. I effectively cut off all the toxic people in my life, including my own horrible mother. I managed to become mentally stable and it’s weird, because I’ve never been mentally stable before. I don’t feel like it’s inherently better, just more in line with my own goals.

I don’t take my emotions or episodes personality. I very seldom get burnt out and most importantly, I no longer feel riddled in guilt or shame about who I am. My mind is simply calm and focused. It’s like my brain has been upgraded or something. The other good thing is that I no longer desire the rebellion from my past. I’ve got more than enough interesting stories to tell, in addition to the lifetime supply of cute snapchat selfies I have of myself and with my girlfriend. I’ve produced all the offensive comedy I could possibly have ever wanted to produce and I feel as if I have remained authentic to myself in so many different ways.

One of the big worries about becoming mentally stable is that you think your ambition and character will disappear, however I can ascertain that it only gets strong. To actually have control over yourself, is a completely phenomenal thing. With my mental health in order, I thought it might be a good idea to get back into creating YouTube comedy. I wasn’t entirely sure how it would turn out, however I no longer wanted to live in fear, nor care about what others thought.

While it started out fine, it slowly evolved into a kind of pure hatred and contempt for humanity. It’s hard to describe, however maybe it’s was subconscious. The videos no longer made any sense, but maybe that was by design. Maybe the intention was the create something incoherent and ugly? Regardless, it wasn’t contributing towards my mental health and so I stopped. Still, it’s glad I’m something I did. I think expression is an important thing, regardless of what it produces.

Largely, this year has been spent studying coding and is a process I hope to improve upon for many years to come. Literally, I will code any chance I can.
